 It is always a marvel to look at someone who just gets something: Who just looks at a problem and sees the solution. Who effortlessly replicates a move that was only shown once or applies a technique in sparring directly after learning it. And it is very easy to feel dumb in comparison, struggling to achieve the same thing with full effort and still falling short.

It is frustrating, exhausting, degrading. It is also lovely. There is so much potential in these moments and so much power in the simple step of deciding to stay with them instead of copping out. And in the decision to return to the same situation a few times. 

And sometimes it does not work, sometimes it is too much. But I have not found a better way. Our ecological nature demands the sacrifice of perfection for growth.
